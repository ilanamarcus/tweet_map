The Right To Be Forgotten

by Ilana Marcus

The right to be forgotten is the notion that people have a right to suppress certain types of information about themselves from search engine listings, especially when that information is inaccurate or no longer relevant. This is also known as the right to delist. This concept pits freedom of expression and access to information against individuals' right to privacy. Clarification of the right to be forgotten in different international jurisdictions is important due to the global nature of search engine data. Different countries apply the concept as a right to varying degrees. The United States is notorious for its emphasis on free speech above privacy rights, but most other countries diverge from this policy.

There is a range of approaches to this issue. Some states require that search engines remove references to defamatory, private, or irrelevant information. Some have determined that the responsibility does not lie with search engines, but with the sites hosting the content in question, and have ruled that those sites must remove or update that information. Others determined that the right to be forgotten has no place in their national law.
